BTSI Lab Systems Integration : 2021-05-26 Meeting notes

Created by Paul Rabahy, last updated on May 26, 2021

Date

26 May 2021

The goal of this meeting is to discuss the safety systems in the lab primarily focusing on the gas detection sensor and its integration into out Test Automation System (iTest).

The three areas of risk that I see are

  1. Injury to lab personnel
  2. Damage to the DUT
  3. Damage to lab equipment

Questions that I would like to answer

  1. Are the gas sensors sufficient to mitigate the risks listed above without integration with the TAS?
  2. Can integration with the TAS improve the reactions to gas being detected?
  3. What actions should the TAS take in response to gas being detected?
  4. How do users need to interact with the gas sensors?

Discussion items

What is the difference between a pack sitting in the lab and in a chamber?

  • Area gas sensors have been installed.
  • Door open policy should make venting more visible.
  • What about packs that are in crates?

Other safety systems

  • Safety TCs (Set to 85°C default)
    • Chad would like to see fewer thermocouples
    • Investigate chamber thermocouples instead of on the pack
    • What is the temperature delta from the pack surface ↔ pack internals ↔ chamber ambient?
  • Packs have thermistors in them
    • The communication to the pack isn't reliable
  • There are 2 light trees
    • This can be confusing
    • Andy's team is working on removing the chamber light stack and leaving the gas sensor stack

Gas sensors

  • Cortek system records 3 signals (Malfunction, CO, LEL)
    • Alerts security.
    • Alerts Tony M.
    • Turns on Lights/Siren at the chamber.
      • Significantly louder than anything else in the lab
  • Has the ability to integrate into a TAS
    • TAS integration will not impact the Cortek reaction.
  • Can we demonstrate this system?
    • Should we take a recording of the system triggering for training purposes?

TAS Integration

  • The TAS system can shutdown the Chiller, Chamber, and Cycler.
  • The TAS system will shutdown an entire area (North lab, South lab, Development area)

Action items

  • Any test with active chemistry must have an integration with the TAS that shuts down all energy sources to the DUT
  • Take a recording of the system triggering for training purposes.
