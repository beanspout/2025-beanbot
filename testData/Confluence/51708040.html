BTSI Lab Systems Integration : GBSL-W & PATAC Test Automation Software Agreement

Created by Justin Hermann on May 14, 2020

Introduction

Based on the outcome of the Jan’19 PATAC and Warren Global Battery Systems Lab (GBSL-W) workshop, the major responsibilities of the GBSL-W team in regards to test automation software are detailed in this document. This list is intended to offer some clarity to the ‘EXHIBITS to AGREEMENT FOR DEVELOPMENT OF BEV3 PROPULSION SYSTEM’ document (CDA), which provides minimal details specific to test automation software, as highlighted in Figures 1 and 2 below.

Figure 1. GBSL-W responsibility for test automation software



Figure 2. GBSL-W responsibility for test automation software

Major Responsibilities

  1. Establish a secure method for orderly sharing of electronic test sequences and related files including test data.
  2. Delivery of GBSL-W test sequences that are required for execution of BEV3 applications as defined in the CDA per Figures 1 and 2. These test sequences will be designed to operate with the most current testing equipment present within the GBSL-W. Any test sequences and related documentation and/or electronic files needed for programs outside of the CDA or for equipment other than that being used at the GBSL-W will not be delivered.

    3. Deliver test sequences developed by the GBSL-W that are not described in Tables 1 or 2 but are required for in-scope testing.
    4. Deliver test equipment drivers developed by the GBSL-W that are required for in-scope testing.
  3. Drive commonality between GBSL-W and PATAC test sequences.
    1. Collaborate with PATAC test engineering to capture and track bug and new feature requests.
    2. Use the GBSL-W-hosted Jira system to document, track and resolve issues. Note: test sequences and related electronic files will need to be delivered outside of the Jira system unless a shared instance of Jira is established.
    3. As needed, lead a periodic joint change control board forum to prioritize and manage issue resolution.
    4. As needed, provide periodic updates for any test sequences and equipment drivers that are delivered to PATAC.
    5. As needed, hotfixes should be provided to address safety concerns and bugs affecting critical functionality.
  4. When possible, and as needed, verify the functionality of test sequences in the GBSL-W prior to delivery to PATAC
  5. Work to train PATAC test engineering personnel with the intent that PATAC can create, verify and maintain new test sequences that are not common with GBSL-W or associated GMW specifications.
    1. Host PATAC personnel in the GBSL-W for hands-on training. Note: Training was completed within the 2019 calendar year.

Timeline of project plan

The responsibilities outlined in this document are expected to apply across the BEV3 co-development period as described in the referenced CDA.
